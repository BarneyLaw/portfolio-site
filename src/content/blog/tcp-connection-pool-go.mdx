Because I wanted to understand what `database/sql` is actually doing under the
hood, I wrote a small TCP connection pool in Go from scratch.

The core is a buffered channel of connections acting as a semaphore: `Get`
pulls one (or dials a new one up to a cap), `Put` returns it, and a background
reaper closes connections idle past a deadline. Writing it made the standard
library's pool behaviour — and its sharp edges around context cancellation —
much less mysterious.
